Privacy Policy

Yearworm · last updated 2 July 2026
Short version: Yearworm collects nothing about you. There are no accounts, no ads, no analytics, and no trackers. Everything the game remembers stays on your own device.

What we collect

Nothing. Yearworm has no user accounts and no sign-in. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information, and we do not use analytics, advertising, or tracking of any kind.

Data stored on your device

To let you resume a game and keep your settings, the app saves a few things in your browser's local storage (on your device only): your current game state, any custom decks you build, difficulty preference, and personal-best scores. This data never leaves your device and is not visible to us. You can clear it any time by clearing the app's site data.

Voluntary correction reports

If you correct a song's release year, you can optionally tap a button to report that correction to us so we can fix it for everyone. Such a report contains only the song title, artist, the old and new year, and the app version — nothing about you or your device.

Third-party services

Playing the game makes ordinary web requests to two external services:

  • Apple iTunes — to search for songs and stream their 30-second preview clips.
  • Google Fonts — to load the app's typography.

As with any website, these services receive the standard technical information a browser sends with a request (such as your IP address). Yearworm sends them no personal data of its own. Their handling of such requests is governed by their own privacy policies.

Children

Yearworm is a general-audience music game and does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date above.
